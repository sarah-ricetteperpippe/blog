This page shows every editorial component in action. Use it as a reference when writing a recipe or guide: find the block you need, copy the snippet, swap in your content.

The components are available **automatically** in every `.mdx` file under `src/content/blog/{it,en,fr}/` and `src/content/academy/{it,en,fr}/`. No need to import them at the top of the file.

## Figure with side text — ratio portrait

The image takes ~36% of the width, the text fills the rest. Suited to vertical photos.

<Figure src="/images/demo/portrait.svg" alt="Amber caramel" side="right" ratio="portrait" caption="Amber, not burnt — the right caramel.">

Melt the sugar over medium heat in a non-stick pan. No water, no spoon: just sugar and heat. At first nothing seems to happen, then the edges start to liquefy.

It's the most nerve-racking moment of the recipe, but it's also where the golden rule is: don't touch. The more you stir the caramel, the more you risk crystallising it.

</Figure>

## TwoColumn — ingredients next to method

Two-column text layout. Use two `<div slot="left">` and `<div slot="right">`. Note the blank line after the opening `<div>` and before the closing `</div>` — MDX needs it to process the content as markdown.

<TwoColumn ratio="1/2">
  <div slot="left">

  ### Ingredients
  - 200g (7.1 oz) sugar
  - 4 large eggs
  - 500ml (16.9 fl oz) whole milk
  - 1 vanilla pod
  - a pinch of salt

  </div>
  <div slot="right">

  ### Method
  1. Make the caramel by melting the sugar over medium heat.
  2. Whisk the eggs with a pinch of salt, without aerating them.
  3. Warm the milk with the vanilla just up to a simmer.
  4. Pour the milk onto the eggs while stirring slowly.
  5. Strain the mixture and pour it into the caramelised mould.
  6. Bake in a water bath at 160°C (320°F) for 45–50 minutes.

  </div>
</TwoColumn>

## Aside — tip, warning, note variants

Side boxes for tricks, warnings and annotations.

<Aside variant="tip" title="Grandma's trick">

If the onion is about to burn, add a spoon of water and lower the heat. It stews instead of drying out.

</Aside>

<Aside variant="warning" title="Watch out">

Caramel easily exceeds 180°C (356°F): use a pan with a long handle and keep your hands away from the rim. If it splashes on your skin, cool it immediately under cold water.

</Aside>

<Aside variant="nota" title="Fun fact">

The Italian term "soffritto" comes from "sotto-friggere": cooking at a lower temperature than proper frying, to extract flavour without browning too much.

</Aside>

```mdx
</Aside>
```

## Component summary

| Component | When to use it |
|---|---|
| `<Figure>` | Single photo, optionally with side text in 2 columns |
| `<Gallery>` | 2 or 3 photos side by side in a grid |
| `<Pullquote>` | Large phrase pulled from the body |
| `<TwoColumn>` | Two text blocks side by side (e.g. ingredients vs method) |
| `<Aside>` | Box for tips, warnings or fun facts |

For real images, save them in `public/images/<recipe-name>/` and reference them with `src="/images/<recipe-name>/photo.jpg"`.
