This page shows each editorial component in action. Use it as a reference when writing a recipe or guide: find the block you need, copy the snippet, replace the content.

The components are available **automatically** in each `.mdx` file of `src/content/blog/{en,en,fr}/` and `src/content/academy/{en,en,fr}/`. There is no need to import them at the top of the file.

## Figures with text beside them - ratio portrait

The picture takes ~36% of the width, the text fills the rest. Suitable for portrait photos.

<Figure src="/images/demo/portrait.svg" alt="Amber caramel" side="right" ratio="portrait" caption="Amber, not burnt - the right caramel.">

Melt the sugar over medium heat in a non-stick pan. No water, no spoon: just sugar and heat. At first nothing seems to happen, then the edges start to liquefy.

It is the most anxious moment in the recipe, but it is also the one where the golden rule is: don't touch. The more you stir the caramel, the more you risk it crystallising.

</Figure>.


## TwoColumn - ingredients next to the process

Two-column text layout. Use two `<div slot="left">` and `<div slot="right">`. Note the blank line after the opening `<div>` and before the closing `</div>` - this is for MDX to process the content as markdown.

<TwoColumn ratio="1/2">
  <div slot="left">

  ### Ingredients
  - 200g (7.1 oz) sugar
  - 4 large eggs
  - 500ml (16.9 fl oz) whole milk
  - 1 vanilla pod
  - a pinch of salt

  </div>
  <div slot="right">

  ### Procedure
  1. Prepare the caramel by melting the sugar over medium heat.
  2. Beat the eggs with a pinch of salt, without whipping them.
  3. Heat the milk with the vanilla until it comes to the boil.
  4. Pour the milk over the eggs, stirring slowly.
  5. Strain the mixture and pour it into the caramelised mould.
  6. Bake in a bain-marie at 160°C (320°F) for 45-50 minutes.

  </div>.
</TwoColumn>.

``mdx
<TwoColumn ratio="1/2">
  <div slot="left">

  ### Ingredients
  - 200g (7.1 oz) sugar
  - 4 large eggs

  </div>
  <div slot="right">

  1. Prepare the caramel

  </div>.
</TwoColumn>
```

## Aside - tip variants, warning, note

Side boxes for tips, warnings and notes.

<Aside variant="tip" title="Grandma's trick">

If the onion is in danger of burning, add a tablespoon of water and lower the heat. It stews and does not dry out.

</Aside>.

<Aside variant="warning" title="Warning">

Caramel easily rises above 180°C (356°F): use a pan with a long handle and do not bring your hands near the edge. If it splashes on your skin, cool immediately under cold water.

</Aside>.

<Aside variant="note" title="Curiosity">

The term 'soffritto' comes from 'under-frying': cooking at a lower temperature than real frying, to extract flavour without browning too much.

</Aside>

``mdx
<Aside variant="tip" title="Grandma's trick">

Text of the tip, processed as markdown.

</Aside>
```

## Summary of components

| Component | When to use it |
|---|---|
| `<Figures>` | Single photo, possibly with text next to it in 2 columns |
| `<Gallery>` | 2 or 3 photos side by side in a grid |
| `<Pullquote>` | Large sentence extracted from the text |
| `<TwoColumn>` | Two text blocks side by side (e.g. ingredients vs. process) |
| | `<Aside>` | Box for tricks, warnings or trivia |

For real images, save them in `public/images/<recipe-name>/` and reference them with `src="/images/<recipe-name>/photo.jpg"`.
